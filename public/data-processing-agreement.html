Data Processing Agreement

LaunchSession is provided by LaunchNexus Ltd (company no. 17333693), 14 Souldern Street, Watford, United Kingdom, WD18 0EU

This is the document most likely to need a solicitor's attention, given it governs processing of children's and safeguarding data at scale across multiple customer organisations.

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between LaunchNexus Ltd ("Processor", "we") and the customer organisation identified in its LaunchSession account ("Controller", "you") and applies whenever we process personal data on your behalf in connection with the LaunchSession Service. Where there is any conflict between this DPA and the Terms of Service on data protection matters, this DPA prevails.

1. Definitions

Terms such as "personal data", "processing", "controller", "processor", "data subject", "special category data", and "personal data breach" have the meanings given in UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 ("Data Protection Law").

"Customer Data" means personal data processed by the Processor on behalf of the Controller through the Service, including data relating to children/young people, parents/carers, staff, and volunteers.

2. Roles

2.1 The parties agree that, in relation to Customer Data, the Controller is the data controller and the Processor is the data processor, for the purposes of Data Protection Law.

2.2 The Processor will process Customer Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller (including as set out in the Terms of Service and this DPA, and as reasonably necessary to provide the Service), unless required to do otherwise by law, in which case the Processor will inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits this.

3. Nature and purpose of processing

3.1 Subject matter: provision of the LaunchSession software platform for youth and community organisation administration.

3.2 Duration: for the term of the Controller's subscription, plus the post-termination export/retention period set out in the Terms of Service.

3.3 Nature and purpose of processing: storage, organisation, retrieval, and display of Customer Data within the Service so the Controller can manage sessions, registers, safeguarding records, risk assessments, volunteers, consents, and related administrative functions.

3.4 Categories of data subjects: children and young people participating in the Controller's activities; their parents/carers; the Controller's staff; and volunteers.

3.5 Categories of personal data: contact and identity details; attendance records; safeguarding and welfare notes; health/medical information relevant to participation (special category data); consent records; vetting/DBS status of staff and volunteers; and, where uploaded by the Controller, photographs or media.

4. Processor obligations

The Processor will:

4.1 process Customer Data only on the Controller's documented instructions, including with regard to international transfers, unless required otherwise by law;

4.2 ensure persons authorised to process Customer Data are subject to a duty of confidentiality;

4.3 implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (see Schedule 1);

4.4 not engage a sub-processor without the Controller's prior general authorisation (see clause 6) and will impose data protection obligations on any sub-processor no less protective than those in this DPA;

4.5 assist the Controller, insofar as reasonably possible, in responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability) relating to Customer Data;

4.6 assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with its obligations regarding security of processing, breach notification, data protection impact assessments, and consultation with supervisory authorities, taking into account the nature of processing and information available to the Processor;

4.7 at the Controller's choice, delete or return all Customer Data at the end of the provision of the Service, save to the extent retention is required by law, in accordance with the export/retention period in the Terms of Service;

4.8 make available to the Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality.

5. Personal data breach notification

5.1 The Processor will notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware, of any personal data breach affecting Customer Data.

5.2 That notification will describe, to the extent then known: the nature of the breach; categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected; likely consequences; and measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including mitigating possible adverse effects. Where full information is not available within 72 hours, the Processor will provide it in phases without undue further delay.

6. Sub-processors

6.1 The Controller provides general authorisation for the Processor to engage sub-processors to provide the Service (for example, cloud hosting, database, and transactional email providers).

6.2 The current list of sub-processors is: Supabase (database, authentication, and file storage), Vercel (application hosting and content delivery), and Resend (transactional email delivery). See Schedule 2 for processing locations.

6.3 The Processor will notify the Controller of any intended addition or replacement of a sub-processor, giving the Controller the opportunity to object on reasonable data-protection grounds.

7. International transfers

Where Customer Data is transferred outside the UK, the Processor will ensure an appropriate safeguard is in place, such as the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, an adequacy regulation, or another lawful transfer mechanism.

As of the date of this DPA: Supabase processes Customer Data in the EU (Ireland), which benefits from the UK's adequacy arrangements with the EU and requires no additional transfer safeguard. Vercel processes data in the US, which is an international transfer requiring a safeguard — the Processor will ensure Vercel is bound by the UK International Data Transfer Addendum (or equivalent SCCs) before Customer Data is processed there. The safeguard status of Resend's processing location will be confirmed once its region is known (see Schedule 2).

8. Special category and children's data

Given the nature of the Service, the parties acknowledge that Customer Data may include special category data (e.g. health information) and data relating to children. The Processor will apply the technical and organisational measures in Schedule 1 with particular regard to this heightened sensitivity, and will not use such data for any purpose other than providing the Service.

9. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations and exclusions set out in the Terms of Service, save that nothing limits liability for a party's own breach of Data Protection Law where such liability cannot lawfully be excluded.

Schedule 1 — Technical and organisational security measures

  • Role-based access control, with access scoped to a user's own organisation via row-level security at the database layer, preventing cross-organisation data access.
  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS) and at rest.
  • Authentication controls for all user accounts, with administrative functions restricted to authorised roles.
  • Logging of key administrative actions (e.g. account and organisation deletions).
  • Regular review of access control policies following schema or feature changes.
  • Defined data retention and secure deletion procedures on account termination.
  • Staff access to production data restricted to what is necessary for support and maintenance.
