Privacy Policy

LaunchSession is provided by LaunchNexus Ltd (company no. 17333693), 14 Souldern Street, Watford, United Kingdom, WD18 0EU

LaunchNexus Ltd ("we", "us", "our") provides LaunchSession, a platform used by youth and community organisations. This policy explains how we handle personal data, and — because LaunchSession is used to record data about children, volunteers, and staff on behalf of organisations — it is split into two parts covering two different relationships.

A note on scope, read this first: In relation to data about your organisation's account holders (the staff and admins who sign up and manage the account), we are the data controller. In relation to data your organisation enters into the platform about children, young people, parents/carers, and volunteers, your organisation is the data controller and we act only as a data processor, under the terms of our Data Processing Agreement. This split matters and is not just a formality — conflating the two is one of the most common mistakes in SaaS privacy policies and it matters more here given the platform handles safeguarding data.

Part A — Organisations and account holders (we are the controller)

What we collect

  • Account and billing details: organisation name, admin contact name, email, phone number, billing address.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser/device type, log data, and usage analytics related to how the account uses the Service.
  • Support communications.

Why we collect it and our lawful basis

  • To provide, maintain, and bill for the Service (performance of a contract).
  • To respond to support requests and communicate service updates (performance of a contract / legitimate interests).
  • To improve the Service, including aggregated and anonymised usage analysis (legitimate interests).
  • To comply with legal and accounting obligations (legal obligation).

Who we share it with

  • Sub-processors who help us run the Service (hosting, database, email delivery) — see the current list in our Data Processing Agreement.
  • Professional advisers (accountants, auditors) where necessary.
  • Regulators or authorities where required by law.

We do not sell organisation or account holder data to third parties.

Retention

We retain account and billing data for the duration of the subscription and for a reasonable period afterwards to meet accounting, tax, and legal obligations (typically up to 6 years for financial records).

Part B — Data entered by organisations about children, parents/carers, staff, and volunteers (your organisation is the controller, we are the processor)

What may be recorded

Depending on which modules your organisation uses, this can include: names and contact details of children/young people and their parents/carers; attendance and registers; safeguarding notes and "cause for concern" records; risk assessment information; health/medical information relevant to sessions (e.g. allergies, conditions); volunteer details, DBS/vetting status; consent records; and photos or media where the organisation has uploaded them.

Some of this — health data, and data revealing matters like safeguarding concerns — is special category data under UK GDPR, and children's data is subject to additional protection under the ICO's Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code) expectations.

Our role

We process this data strictly on the instructions of the organisation that entered it, as set out in our Data Processing Agreement. We do not use this data for our own marketing, profiling, or any purpose beyond providing the Service. We do not sell it. Access within the platform is scoped by row-level security so that one organisation cannot see another organisation's data.

Your rights, if data about you is held on the platform

If you are a parent, carer, young person, staff member, or volunteer and believe an organisation holds data about you on LaunchSession, your data protection rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability, as applicable) should be exercised with that organisation directly, since they are the data controller and hold the context needed to respond. We will support organisations in fulfilling these requests where technically necessary.

Security measures

We apply access controls (role-based permissions, row-level data isolation between organisations), encryption in transit, and encryption at rest for stored data, alongside regular review of database access policies. Full technical and organisational measures are described in the Data Processing Agreement.

International transfers

Where any sub-processor is located outside the UK, we ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place (such as the UK's International Data Transfer Addendum or an adequacy finding) before data is transferred.

Cookies and similar technologies

See our separate Cookie Policy for details of cookies and local/session storage used by the Service.

Children's privacy on our marketing site

Our public marketing website (launchsession.co.uk) is directed at organisations, not children, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children through the marketing site itself. Data about children is only ever entered by an organisation into the secured application, under Part B above.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time; material changes will be notified as described in our Terms of Service. The "last updated" date at the top of this page shows when it was last revised.
